Harvest Bowl

Ingredients

  • 4 teaspoons cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds (toasted and cracked)
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon pepper
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 ounces arugula
  • 1/2 Granny Smith apple
  • 1 cup roasted sweet potatoes
  • 2 oz feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons dried cranberries
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 1 large egg white
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon caraway seeds, crushed
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/3 cup sunflower seeds
  • 1/3 cup pepitas
  • 2 tablespoons sesame seeds
  • 2 tablespoons nigella seeds

Instructions

  1. Brittle:
    1. Adjust oven rack to upper middle portion and heat oven to 300 degrees.
    2. Line 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper and spray parchment with vegetable oil spray.
    3. Whisk maple syrup, egg white, oil, soy sauce, caraway seeds, salt, and pepper together in large bowl.
    4. Stir in oats, sunflower seeds, pepitas, sesame seeds, and nigella seeds until well combined.
    5. Transfer mixture to prepared pan and spread into an even layer. Using stiff metal spatula press mixture until very compact.
    6. Bake until golden brown and fragrant, 45 to 55 minutes, rotating pan halfway through baking. In the same oven bake sweet potatoes.
    7. Transfer pan to wire rack and let brittle cool completely, about 1 hour. Break cooked brittle into pieces of desired size, discarding parchment. (Brittle can be stored in an airtight container at room temperature for 1 month.)
  2. Harvest Bowl directions:
    1. Dressing: Whisk vinegar, water, mustard, caraway seeds, salt, and pepper together in a bowl. While whisking constantly, slowly drizzle in oil until combined.
    2. Toss arugula just before serving in dressing, season with salt and pepper.
    3. Divide among two bowls and top with sweet potatoes and apple.
    4. Drizzle with remaining dressing and sprinkle with feta, cranberries, and brittle.
    5. Note: Add wild rice to the dish to make it heartier.

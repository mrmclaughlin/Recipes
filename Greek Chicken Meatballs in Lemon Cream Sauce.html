Greek Chicken Meatballs in Lemon Cream Sauce

Prep Time: 20 MIN | Cook Time: 25 MIN | Total Time: 45 MIN | YIELD: SERVES 5-6

Delicious Greek chicken meatballs in a creamy dreamy lemon sauce. These meatballs are tender to the core! Nestled in a rich and creamy lemon sauce, they are perfect to serve over pasta, quinoa, or fluffy rice!

Ingredients

Meatballs:

  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon EACH: lemon zest AND chopped parsley
  • ¾ cup panko breadcrumbs
  • ⅓ cup grated yellow onions
  • ½ cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ teaspoon dried oregano (a little more if it’s fresh)
  • 1 ¼ pound lean ground chicken

Lemon Olive Sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons salted cold butter, cubed
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 ½ cups low sodium chicken stock
  • ½ cup green olives (or use ¼ cup capers!)
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest + ⅓ cup lemon juice
  • 2 egg yolks
  • ⅛ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg

Instructions

  1. MEATBALLS: In a medium bowl, using a fork, mix together all the ingredients except the ground chicken. Add 1 teaspoon of salt and ½ teaspoon of pepper. When combined, add the ground chicken and mix until *just* combined. Roll into 24-28 meatballs using a cookie scoop or about 1 ½ tablespoon each.
  2. COOK: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Drizzle in a little oil. Add the meatballs to the skillet but do not overcrowd. Cook in batches if needed. Sear the meatballs for 5-7 minutes, flipping to brown all sides evenly, until cooked through. Remove to a plate.
  3. SAUCE: Add 2 tablespoons butter to the skillet with the minced garlic for 30 seconds, stirring. Add the flour and whisk. Slowly drizzle in the chicken broth, then simmer for 3 minutes until thickened. Lower the heat. Whisk together the lemon juice and egg yolks, then gradually mix in a bit of the sauce. Add this mixture back to the skillet. Cook for 4-6 minutes until thickened. Stir in the nutmeg and lemon zest. Adjust seasoning. Add the last tablespoon of butter, the green olives, and meatballs back to the skillet. Warm through.
  4. Serve with fried bread, over buttered orzo, or rice!

How to Make Jun Tea (Honey Kombucha)

Jun, also called "champagne of kombucha", is a honey-based kombucha. Here is a recipe to make jun tea at home!

4.19 of 11 ratings

PREPARATION TIME: 15 minutes

FERMENTATION: 10 days

COURSE: Boissons

SERVINGS: 3 L

EQUIPMENT:

  • 1 wide mouth glass jar (1 gallon or more)
  • 1 Cloth and rubber band
  • 4 Pressure-resistant bottles
  • 1 Funnel

INGREDIENTS

  • 10 cups filtered water
  • 1 jun scoby and its liquid starter (about 1Â½ cup)
  • 2 tbsp. tea
  • 1 cup honey

STEPS

  1. Preparation
  2. Place the tea bag in the jar.
  3. Pour 500 ml of boiling water and let infuse for 15 minutes. Remove the tea bag.
  4. Add the honey and stir until it's dissolved.
  5. Add the rest of the cold water, until you get 3 litres.
  6. Add the jun scoby with its liquid culture.
  7. Cover the container with the cloth and the rubber band.
  8. Fermentation
  9. Let it ferment for 5 to 12 days at room temperature, away from direct sunlight.
  10. As of day 5, taste once a day. As soon as the acidity is to your liking, it is time to bottle.
  11. Set aside the scoby along with some jun (about 500 ml) for your next recipe. Store in the fridge.
  12. Bottling
  13. Transfer the jun into pressure resistant bottles. Fill to within 5 cm of the rim and close tightly.
  14. If you want a fizzier jun, let the bottles ferment at room temperature for 2 to 4 days.
  15. Put in the fridge as soon as the jun is fizzy (be careful with the pressure).

NOTES

  • Important! Don't forget a room temperature bottle, as the pressure continues to build up day after day. You risk painting your ceiling, or worse, exploding your bottle.
  • Jun can be stored in the fridge for several months.

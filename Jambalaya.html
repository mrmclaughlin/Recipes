Jambalaya (Modified)

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 1 pound of andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1.5 pounds of boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14.5 ounces) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups of chicken broth
  • 1 cup of long-grain white rice
  • 1 tablespoon of paprika
  • 1 tablespoon of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of dried thyme
  • 1/2 tablespoon of cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley

Directions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced andouille sausage to the pot and cook until browned, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chicken to the pot and cook until browned, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the sausage and chicken from the pot and set aside.
  4. Add the chopped onion, green bell pepper, and celery to the pot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant.
  6. Add the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, rice, paprika, oregano, thyme, cayenne pepper, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
  7. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and most of the liquid has been absorbed.
  8. Add the cooked sausage and chicken back to the pot. Stir to combine.
  9. Cook for an additional 5-7 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  10. Sprinkle with chopped fresh parsley and serve hot.
